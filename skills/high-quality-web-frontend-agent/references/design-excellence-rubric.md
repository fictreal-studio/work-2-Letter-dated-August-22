# Design Excellence Rubric

Judge design excellence separately from defect absence. A site is not high quality merely because it is accessible, responsive, and free of broken links. It must also show authored visual judgment at the level expected from an excellent professional studio or mature in-house design team.

## Design Core

Score these categories from 0 to 5 with rendered evidence.

| Category | Weight | A score of 5 means |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Art Direction And Owner Specificity | 14 | The concept is unmistakably tied to this owner, purpose, history, audience, and emotional register. Replacing the name or colors would break the design logic. |
| Composition And Visual Hierarchy | 12 | First attention, second attention, reading path, density, whitespace, scale, contrast, and section transitions are intentional at every required width. |
| Typography And Editorial Rhythm | 10 | Type choice, hierarchy, Japanese measure, line breaks, rhythm, punctuation, labels, and display/body contrast feel authored rather than merely readable. |
| Imagery And Graphic Language | 8 | Photography, illustration, diagrams, motifs, crops, and treatments form one specific visual language and materially strengthen meaning. |
| Responsive Art Direction | 8 | Mobile, tablet, and desktop are separately composed; hierarchy, crop, density, order, and interaction adapt rather than merely shrink. |
| Detail And Finish | 8 | Alignment, optical spacing, borders, states, transitions, iconography, surfaces, microcopy, and edge cases remain coherent under close inspection. |

Design Core total: 60 points.

## Score Meaning

- **5 - Exceptional:** distinctive, confident, coherent, and publication-ready; choices reward close inspection.
- **4 - Strong:** professional and specific with only localized refinement opportunities.
- **3 - Competent:** usable and coherent but conventional, under-authored, repetitive, or visually replaceable.
- **2 - Weak:** generic direction, inconsistent hierarchy, unresolved typography, or compressed responsive composition.
- **1 - Poor:** major visual incoherence or obvious template/prototype quality.
- **0 - Absent:** category is missing or unusable.

Do not award 4 or 5 without evidence from both the first viewport and at least one content-heavy section. Verify at mobile and desktop. Use 3 as the honest score for a clean but generic implementation.

## Excellence Gates

- Release Ready requires Design Core at least 51/60 and every Design Core category at least 4/5.
- Conditional Release requires Design Core at least 45/60 and no Design Core category below 3.5/5.
- A Design Core category below 3/5 forces Needs Revision even when functional and safety checks pass.
- Generic template quality, interchangeable branding, weak first-view hierarchy, or desktop-only composition is at least P1 when the stated task is premium production work; otherwise record it as P2.

## Review Questions

1. Could this visual system belong only to this owner?
2. Are the first three points of attention intentional at mobile and desktop?
3. Does each section introduce a meaningful compositional idea rather than repeat one card pattern?
4. Do typography and imagery carry meaning, not decoration alone?
5. Does mobile preserve the same confidence and narrative hierarchy as desktop?
6. Would a senior design review identify deliberate choices rather than merely absence of mistakes?
